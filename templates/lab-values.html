üìö Study Tips for Lab Values

  • Understand the "why": Know why values change and what conditions cause abnormalities
  • Memory aids: Use mnemonics to remember critical values and normal ranges
  • Group by system: Study values by body system (cardiac, renal, metabolic, etc.)
  • Clinical context: Connect lab values to patient conditions and nursing interventions
  • Trends matter: Understand rising vs. falling trends and their clinical implications
  • Practice calculations: Be familiar with units and simple conversions
  • Critical values: Memorize critical lab values that require immediate action
  • Spaced repetition: Review regularly to maintain retention
