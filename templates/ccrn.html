üéØ Core Content Areas

Hemodynamics & Cardiology (25%)

  • Cardiovascular physiology: Normal/abnormal heart sounds, coronary circulation, cardiac output
  • Hemodynamic monitoring: CVP, PA catheters, SVR, PVR, MAP calculations
  • Acute coronary syndromes: STEMI, NSTEMI, unstable angina management
  • Cardiogenic shock: Pathophysiology, pharmacological support, mechanical circulatory support
  • Dysrhythmias: Recognition, treatment, ACLS protocols

Pulmonary Systems (20%)

  • Respiratory physiology: Ventilation, perfusion, gas exchange, acid-base balance
  • ARDS: Pathophysiology, lung protective ventilation, management strategies
  • Mechanical ventilation: Modes, settings, weaning protocols, complications
  • Oxygenation assessment: ABG interpretation, SaO2, PaO2/FiO2 ratio
  • Pulmonary embolism: Risk factors, diagnosis, treatment, prevention

Renal/Metabolic Systems (15%)

  • Acute kidney injury: Pathophysiology, staging, management, fluid balance
  • Electrolyte imbalances: Sodium, potassium, calcium, magnesium abnormalities
  • Acid-base disorders: Metabolic/respiratory acidosis and alkalosis
  • Renal replacement therapy: Dialysis, CRRT, indications and complications
  • Diabetes and glycemic control: DKA, HHS, insulin therapy in critical care

Neurological Systems (15%)

  • Acute stroke: Ischemic vs. hemorrhagic, thrombolytics, NIH Stroke Scale
  • Traumatic brain injury: Pathophysiology, ICP management, Glasgow Coma Scale
  • Seizures: Status epilepticus, antiepileptic drugs, management
  • Neuro assessment: GCS, pupil assessment, neuro checks
  • Meningitis and encephalitis: Recognition, treatment, antibiotic selection
